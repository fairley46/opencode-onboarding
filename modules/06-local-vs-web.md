# Module 4: Local vs Web Execution

## Why This Matters

One of the most important beginner questions is: "Where is this actually happening?" That is the right question, because location affects trust, permissions, and data handling.

## The Simple Picture

Local means the work is happening close to your device, files, or environment. Web or remote means some capability relies on an outside service. In real use, both can exist in the same workflow.

## Visual

```text
Local side: your machine, your files, local tools
Remote side: hosted model, external API, connected service

Good question: what crosses that boundary?
```

## A Practical Example

You might have:

- a local workspace with documents and configuration files
- a remote model answering your question
- an approved MCP server connecting to a company system

That is one workflow, but it crosses more than one boundary. Good onboarding should help the learner notice that, not hide it.

## Why It Matters In Practice

If a learner knows what is local, what is remote, and what is connected, they can ask better questions:

- what data is being used?
- what leaves my machine?
- what permissions are active right now?

## Safety Check

This is not just a technical detail. It is part of enterprise governance. Learners should never be encouraged to ignore where execution happens.

## What To Remember

Before trusting a workflow, ask where it runs and what it touches.
