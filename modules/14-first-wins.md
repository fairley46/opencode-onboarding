# Module 11: First Useful Workflows

## Why This Matters

Confidence grows faster when the learner leaves with a few clear, repeatable wins instead of one big abstract understanding.

## The Simple Picture

The best beginner workflows are small, reviewable, and easy to explain to someone else.

## Good First Wins

- summarize a document in plain language
- compare two sources and list the differences
- draft a first-pass answer for human review
- retrieve approved information from a safe source

## Why These Work

They show real value without encouraging overreach. They also keep the human in the loop naturally because the output still needs review, judgment, or approval.

## A Practical Example

"Read these two policy summaries. Tell me what changed, what stayed the same, and what I should verify with a person before acting on this."

That is useful, clear, and safe.

## Safety Check

The learner should not leave this onboarding thinking AI is the approver. It is the accelerator.

## What To Remember

The first win should feel useful, not risky.
